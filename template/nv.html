Skin Cancer Type

Nevus (NV)

Nevi (plural of nevus) are very common and can appear anywhere on the body. They can vary in color, shape, and size. Most nevi are harmless and do not require medical intervention. However, it's important to keep an eye on any changes in the appearance of nevi, such as changes in color, shape, or size.

While most nevi are benign, some may have atypical features that could suggest a risk of developing into melanoma. It's recommended to consult a dermatologist if you notice any of the following features in a nevus:

1. Asymmetry: One half of the mole does not match the other half.

2. Varied Colors: The mole has different colors or unevenly distributed colors.

3. Diameter: The mole is larger than 6 millimeters in diameter
.
4. Evolution: The mole is changing in size, shape, or color over time.

Regular self-examinations and professional skin screenings can help identify any changes in moles or other skin lesions. If you have concerns about nevi or any other skin-related issues, it's best to consult with a dermatologist or healthcare professional.
