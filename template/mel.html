Skin Cancer Type

Melanoma (MEL)

Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that develops in the melanocytes, which are the cells that produce melanin, the pigment responsible for skin color. Melanoma is considered the most serious and aggressive form of skin cancer.

Melanoma can develop from existing moles or appear as a new, unusual growth on the skin. It may vary in color, size, and shape, and it can appear anywhere on the body. Melanoma is more likely to spread to other parts of the body compared to other types of skin cancer.

Early detection and treatment are crucial for improving the prognosis of melanoma. Regular skin self-examinations and dermatologist screenings are important for identifying any suspicious growths. If you notice any changes in the color, size, shape, or appearance of a mole or other skin lesion, it's recommended to seek medical attention promptly.

Melanoma can be deadly if not treated early, but with timely intervention, the prognosis can be significantly improved. If you have concerns about melanoma or other skin-related issues, it's best to consult with a dermatologist or healthcare professional.
