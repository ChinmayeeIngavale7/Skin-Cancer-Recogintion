Skin Cancer Type

Vascular Lesions (VASC)

Vascular lesions are abnormalities in the blood vessels that can appear on the skin. These lesions can vary in size, color, and appearance. They are generally categorized into two main types: vascular tumors and vascular malformations.

1. Vascular Tumors: These are growths of blood vessels that are usually benign. They can appear as red or pink raised areas on the skin. Common examples include hemangiomas and cherry angiomas.

2. Vascular Malformations: These are structural abnormalities of blood vessels that are present at birth. They do not undergo rapid growth like tumors and tend to grow at the same rate as the child. Examples include port-wine stains and venous malformations.

It's important to note that while most vascular lesions are benign and do not pose a threat, some may require medical evaluation and treatment, especially if they cause discomfort, bleeding, or other issues. A dermatologist or medical professional can assess the type of vascular lesion and recommend appropriate management.

If you suspect you have a vascular lesion or any other skin-related concern, it's recommended to consult with a dermatologist or healthcare provider for proper evaluation and guidance.
